1 About this Sprint

This is a release sprint. This means that you package things that you have done in other sprints in order to create a release (an assignment delivery). You are expected to integrate what you have learnt previously and test/evaluate your release candidate to maximise your potential for passing the assignment.

2 User Stories covered in this Sprint

  • As a student I want to summarise my understanding of the business case for cloud computing so that I can understand when and how (and when not) to seek a cloud-based solution.
  • As a teacher I want to evaluate the students’ understanding of core business concepts in cloud computing so that I know they fulfil the corresponding course goals.

3 Course Goals covered in this Sprint

Kunskap och förståelse Efter genomförd kurs ska studenten:

  • Ingående kunna redogöra för utmaningar med Big Data-analys
  • Ingående kunna redogöra för olika typer av cloud-plattformar
  • Ingående kunna redogöra för vanliga anledningar att söka sig till en cloudlösning, och utmaningarna med dessa.
  • Ingående kunna resonera om lösningar till vanliga utmaningar med cloudlösningar.

6 Experiential Learning

6.1 Assignment Submission

  • Submit the essay produced in the sprint Assignment Introduction: Cloud and Big Data Business Case to the teachers for marking.
  • The teacher may want to book a time with you to further discuss your sumbission.

Conditions of Satisfaction

When marking the assignment we are looking for the following:

  1. Does the title page contain name and contact information of the author?
  2. Size of the assignment: Is the essay between 5-10 pages?
  3. Are all questions addressed?
  4. Are all answers motivated?
  5. Is the student able to both summarise existing literature and relate to own experiences?
  6. Is the student able to connect and discuss different sources and viewpoints on each topic?

7 Sprint Acceptance Tests

You are done with this assignment when:

  • You have submitted your essay to a teacher for review.
  • You have received a passing grade from the teacher.

You may also:

  • have had a meeting with the teacher to further discuss your submission.
